As we invite robots into places we live and work, I aim to enable them with the same level of efficiency, adaptability, and robustness as humans through natural-world supervision for real-world robot learning. This agenda focuses on resolving three challenges experienced firsthand in field robotics bottlenecking scalable autonomy:

  1. Intuitive Task Understanding. Rewards and objectives can be highly domain-specific or impossible, especially for end-users, to design.
    Robots should be capable of inferring goals from observations of the world and their users.

  2. Continual Physical Understanding. The diversity of the natural world tends to render naive generalization of static, pre-trained features improbable and dangerous.
    Robots should be capable of reasoning and learning when it inevitably finds itself in an unfamiliar situation.

  3. End-to-End Learning. (Not end-to-end acting!) Most, if not all, problems arose at the perception-control interface where hand-designed abstractions were enforced.
    Robots should be trained end-to-end as engineering distinctions and modules blur in challenging settings.
